## The problem

You've tried ChatGPT. Maybe you use it daily. But you're copy-pasting prompts from Twitter, getting inconsistent results, and you have no idea if you're using it well or just getting lucky. The AI tools are powerful — but nobody taught you how to use them systematically.

**AI Fluency changes that.** 4 levels. 20 lessons. No coding required. From your first structured conversation to delegating real work to AI — step by step.

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>Do I need technical skills or coding experience?</summary>

No. This course is designed for knowledge workers, not developers. Every concept is explained in plain language. If you can use email and a web browser, you can do this course.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Which AI tool do I need?</summary>

Any of the major AI assistants works: ChatGPT (free tier), Claude (free tier), or Google Gemini. The course explains the differences and helps you choose. Most lessons work with any tool.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How is this different from watching YouTube tutorials?</summary>

Tutorials show tricks. This course builds skills. Each lesson follows a structured pattern — concept, example, practice. You build on what you learned before. After 4 levels, you don't need tutorials anymore because you understand the principles.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I skip levels?</summary>

Yes. Take the Level Check to find your starting point. If you already prompt deliberately, start at Level 3. If you're already building AI workspaces, start at Level 4.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Is this available in German?</summary>

Yes. Every lesson exists in both English and German. Use the language switcher in the top navigation.
</details>

## Why this exists

The gap between "I've tried ChatGPT" and "I use AI to do better work" is bigger than it looks. Most people stay at the chatbot stage — asking random questions, getting random results. This course exists because structured AI skills shouldn't require a computer science degree. Built for people who think for a living.
